    Stalls: 1 2  3  4 5

    Depending on where on the property your horse is located, some stalls are bigger than others, some recieve no natural light, some horses have only an outdoor paddock. The paddocks are cheaper but boarders have no stall choice.

    Pastures:  1  2 3 4 5

    The only pastures on property are the fulltime paddocks. There is no turnout at this barn. The full time paddocks do not have any grass, only rocks and dirt, and are not mucked or cleaned. There are too many of these paddocks on property, as well as too many horses. Paddocks feature wooden fencing as well as electric fencing.

    Staff: 1  2  3 4 5

    The only staff on-site is one groom who is responsible for feeding and cleaning up after over 100 horses. The barn manager is the owner of the barn. He addresses any concerns you may have but is not present every day nor does he live on the property.

    Hay/Feed: 1  2  3 4 5

    Horses at El Paso Ranch are all fed one brand of low quality feed. The only other meal plan option is if you choose to buy it yourself, and will be additional to your full board price. Horses are fed twice a day, and are given hay cubes rather than hay.

    The facility is kept relatively clean, although itâ€™s cleanliness is at the boarders control. There is very seldom any additional cleaning done by the barn staff.

    Tackroom: 1 2  3  4 5

    This barn features multiple tackrooms across the property where boarders can store their equipment. Tackrooms are not airconditioned, and are not cleaned unless a boarder chooses to do so.

    Ambience: 1 2  3  4 5

    This barn features a very relaxed, laid back atmosphere. On Friday nights there will often be boarders drinking and riding on property, even though riding areas are not lit. I do not recommend any proffessional riders or students in training to ride here.

    Lounge:  1  2 3 4 5

    There is an outdoor lounge for boarders to relax in. There are no fans and it is not airconditioned, so it is not too pleasant to hang out here. There are various hammocks and sitting areas located around the property but none are indoors.

    Arenas: 1 2  3  4 5

    There is one relatively small arena on property that is composed of grass as well as a lightly sanded border. There is a sounding board in the middle for Paso Fino riders to use. There is no gate to close the arena, making it not completely safe and enclosed. The arena is not lit.
